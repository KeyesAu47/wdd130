History

White water rafting has a rich history that dates back to the early 19th century when adventurous explorers and pioneers first navigated America’s wild rivers. The first documented rafting expedition occurred in 1811 on the Missouri River, led by John Colter, a member of the Lewis and Clark expedition. However, it wasn’t until the 1940s and 1950s that rafting gained popularity as a recreational activity. The invention of inflatable rafts made it accessible to the general public, leading to organized rafting trips and the establishment of commercial rafting companies. Today, white water rafting is a thrilling outdoor sport enjoyed worldwide, celebrated for its blend of adventure and teamwork amidst stunning natural landscapes.
